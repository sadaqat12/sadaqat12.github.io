Trends

Here are a couple of trends that I found interesting!

Distribution of Durations

This graph displays how each pass type has a difference in the times the customers are likely to use the bike for. Customers who just walk up to a bike without a pass, are likely to use it for longer than say someone in the staff might use it for. This is likely because walk-up customers likely don't need a bike on a daily basis and just use it occasionally when they need to go farther. While staff would likely just move the bikes in between stations. Furthermore, we can see that Flex-Pass clients are likely to use the bikes for a much shorter amount of time than a monthly pass user. We can assume from the data that the monthly pass user's are the heaviest regular users of the bikes.

Distribution of Pass Types

From this bar graph we can further support our previous claim that monthly users are the heaviest regular users of the bikes. This can be seen in the chart as there are 81,304 monthly users compared to 41,224 walk-up, 9,517 flex-pass, and 382 staff clients. I was able to calculate the exact number of clients in each category using an R algorithm. This answers the 4th challenge question, which asks how many customers include bike sharing as a regular part of their commute. For this all we need to do is add the number of clients for each pass category excluding walk-ups. The resulting answer is 91,203 regular customers.

Usage Across Months

A visual in which it is seen how the bike rentals are affected by the time of the year it is. As can be seen, the summer is a lot busier than the winter, which is reasonable as there are likely more tourists in the summer time, and generally more bike friendly temperatures. While in the winter it gets colder, with fewer tourists so the usage of the bikes decreases. The peak time in terms of ridership appears to be in August with about 24,000 riders, as compared to the slowest month of February in which only about 11,500 clients ride the bikes. This also answers the first bonus question as to how the ridership changes across seasons.
